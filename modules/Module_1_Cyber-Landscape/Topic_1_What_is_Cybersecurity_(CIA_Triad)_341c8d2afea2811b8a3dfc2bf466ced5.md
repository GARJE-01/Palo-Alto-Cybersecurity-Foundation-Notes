# Topic 1: What is Cybersecurity (CIA Triad)

## 📌 Definition

The practice of protecting data, systems, and networks from unauthorized access, modification, and disruption.

---

## 🧠 Simple Explanation

Cybersecurity rests on **3 pillars (CIA Triad)**:

- **Confidentiality** → Only authorized people access data
- **Integrity** → Data remains accurate and unaltered
- **Availability** → Data/services accessible when needed

---

## 🌍 Real-World Example

| Example | Pillar |
| --- | --- |
| Strong password protection | Confidentiality |
| Preventing unauthorized data changes | Integrity |
| Website uptime/functionality | Availability |

---

## 💡 Exam & Interview Points

- Most asked: *"Explain CIA Triad with an example"*
- Common mistake: Treating one pillar as more important than others
- All three are **equally critical** — failure in any one compromises the whole system

---

## 🚀 Quick Revision

- ▪ CIA = Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability
- ▪ Foundation of all cybersecurity strategies
- ▪ Each pillar addresses a different security concern
- ▪ Real-world impact: Data leaks (C), wrong decisions (I), business loss (A)
