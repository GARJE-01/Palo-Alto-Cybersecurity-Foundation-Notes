# Topic 6: Types of Threat Vectors

## 🧠 Simple Explanation

**Common entry points for attackers:**

- Phishing (fake emails)
- Spoofing (fake identity/website)
- Social engineering (manipulation)
- Ransomware (encryption for payment)

---

## 🌍 Real-World Example

- Phishing: "Click here to verify OTP" fake email
- Spoofing: Fake bank website that looks authentic

---

## 💡 Exam & Interview Points

- **Stat: 90% of attacks start with phishing**
- Interview question: *"Explain phishing vs. spear phishing"*
    - **Phishing**: Mass attack
    - **Spear phishing**: Targeted attack against specific person

---

## 🔑 Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| **Phishing** | Mass deceptive email to steal credentials |
| **Spear Phishing** | Targeted phishing against individual/organization |
| **Spoofing** | Impersonating legitimate entity |
| **Ransomware** | Malware that locks/encrypts data demanding payment |

---

## 🚀 Quick Revision

- ▪ Entry point = threat vector
- ▪ Most attacks start with human interaction
- ▪ Email = primary attack vector
