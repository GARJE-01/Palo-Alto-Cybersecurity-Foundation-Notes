# Topic 9: NIST Cybersecurity Framework

## 📌 Definition

A structured approach to managing cybersecurity risks using a 5-phase continuous cycle.

---

## 🧠 Simple Explanation

NIST provides a **5-step lifecycle**:

1. **Identify** → Know your assets and risks
2. **Protect** → Implement safeguards
3. **Detect** → Monitor for threats
4. **Respond** → Act quickly on incidents
5. **Recover** → Restore operations

---

## ⚙️ How It Works (Step-by-Step)

1. Identify critical assets and vulnerabilities
2. Protect with access controls, encryption, firewalls
3. Detect threats via monitoring and alerts
4. Respond with incident response procedures
5. Recover by restoring systems and data

---

## 🌍 Real-World Example

**Hospital analogy:**

- Diagnose condition → Identify threat
- Prevent infection → Protect systems
- Monitor vitals → Detect changes
- Treat disease → Respond to incident
- Recovery plan → Recover operations

---

## 💡 Exam & Interview Points

- **VERY COMMON interview question: "Explain NIST framework lifecycle"**
- Key insight: It's **continuous**, not a one-time process
- Used in government and enterprise organizations

---

## 🚀 Quick Revision

- ▪ 5-step continuous security cycle
- ▪ Repeating process (not linear)
- ▪ Foundation for organizational security strategy
