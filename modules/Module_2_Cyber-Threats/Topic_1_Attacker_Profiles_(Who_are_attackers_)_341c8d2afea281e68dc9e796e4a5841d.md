# Topic 1: Attacker Profiles (Who are attackers?)

## 🧠 Simple Explanation

Cybersecurity is not just about systems — it's about **people attacking systems**.

Attackers differ based on:

- **Skill level**
- **Resources**
- **Motivation**

---

## 🔑 Key Attacker Types

### 1. **Cybercriminals**

- **Goal**: 💰 Money
- **Work**: Individually or in gangs
- **Methods**: Ransomware, fraud, data theft
- **Stat**: Today cybercrime = $1 trillion industry

### 2. **Nation-State Attackers**

- **Sponsored by**: Governments
- **Skills**: Highly skilled + well-funded
- **Target**: Power grids, military systems, government data
- **Note**: Most dangerous attackers

### 3. **Hacktivists**

- **Motivation**: Political/social causes
- **Methods**: Website defacing, DDoS attacks
- **Example**: Anonymous

### 4. **Cyberterrorists**

- **Goal**: Fear + destruction
- **Target**: Critical infrastructure, public systems
- **Threat Level**: Very dangerous but less common

### 5. **Script Kiddies**

- **Skill**: Beginners
- **Method**: Use ready-made tools
- **Knowledge**: No deep technical understanding
- **Note**: Low skill but still dangerous

---

## 🌍 Real-World Comparison

| Attacker Type | Motivation | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cybercriminal | Money | Ransomware gang |
| Nation-state | Power/Intelligence | Russia/China attacks |
| Hacktivist | Ideology | Website defacement |
| Script kiddie | Curiosity/Easy targets | Using hacking tools |
| Cyberterrorist | Terror | Critical infrastructure attack |

---

## ❓ Why It Matters

In real cybersecurity jobs:

- **Don't just detect attack** — identify WHO attacked and WHY
- This helps:
    - Predict next attack
    - Choose defense strategy
    - Allocate resources properly

---

## 💡 Exam & Interview Points

**Very common question**: *"Types of attackers in cybersecurity?"*

**Best structured answer**:

- Cybercriminal (money-driven)
- Nation-state (power-driven)
- Hacktivist (ideology-driven)
- Insider (access-driven)
- Script kiddie (curiosity-driven)
