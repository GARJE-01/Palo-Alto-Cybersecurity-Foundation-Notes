# Topic 6: High-Profile Cyberattacks (Real Cases)

## 🧠 Simple Explanation

Cyberattacks are not theory — **they cause real financial loss, data theft, and infrastructure disruption**.

Your module shows real incidents where:

- Millions of users affected
- Companies lost billions
- Critical systems were shut down

---

## 🏢 Major Real-World Cyberattacks

### 1. **Target Attack (2013)**

**What happened**: 40 million credit card details stolen

**How it happened**: Attack came through HVAC vendor system

**Key lesson**: **Third-party/vendor systems = weak point**

**Impact**: $18.5 million settlement, massive reputation damage

---

### 2. **Home Depot Attack (2014)**

**What happened**: 56 million card details stolen

**How it happened**: Malware installed on Point-of-Sale (POS) systems

**Key lesson**: **Unpatched systems = high risk**

**Impact**: $50 million settlement + reputation damage

---

### 3. **Anthem Attack (2015)**

**What happened**: 80 million records leaked (names, SSNs, medical data)

**How it happened**: Used stolen employee credentials

**Key lesson**: **Weak authentication = dangerous**

**Impact**: $100+ million settlement, healthcare system disruption

---

### 4. **SolarWinds Attack (2020)**

**What happened**: Supply chain attack affecting 18,000+ organizations

**How it happened**: Malware inserted into legitimate software update

**Affected**: Government agencies, Fortune 500 companies

**Key lesson**: **Even trusted software can be compromised**

**Impact**: One of largest breaches ever, months of investigation

---

### 5. **Colonial Pipeline Attack (2021)**

**What happened**: Ransomware attack on fuel supply infrastructure

**How it happened**: Compromised password led to system access

**Impact**: Fuel shortages across US East Coast, public disruption

**Key lesson**: **Cyberattack affects real-world infrastructure**

**Real impact**: Gas station shortages, panic buying

---

### 6. **Capital One Data Breach (2019)**

**What happened**: 100+ million customers affected

**How it happened**: Cloud misconfiguration + unpatched vulnerability

**Affected data**: Names, SSNs, credit card numbers

**Key lesson**: **Cloud misconfiguration = major risk**

**Impact**: $100 million fine, CIO prosecuted

---

## 📊 Common Patterns Across All Attacks

| Common Weakness | Examples | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Weak passwords | Anthem | Strong MFA |
| Unpatched systems | Home Depot | Regular updates |
| Misconfiguration | Capital One | Security reviews |
| Vendor access | Target | Third-party audits |
| Social engineering | Many | Employee training |

---

## ⚙️ Think Like Security Analyst

When analyzing real attacks, ask:

1. **Where did attack start?** (Entry point)
2. **What was the weak point?** (Root cause)
3. **How to prevent it?** (Control needed)
4. **How long undetected?** (Detection gap)
5. **What was final damage?** (Impact)

---

## ❓ Why This Matters

✅ **Real attacks teach us lessons**

✅ **Show actual attack methods** (not theoretical)

✅ **Reveal common weaknesses** companies share

✅ **Demonstrate real-world impact** of breaches

## 💡 Exam & Interview Points

**Common question**: *"Give example of a cyberattack"*

**Best answer** (with context):

> "Target's 2013 attack compromised 40 million card details through a vendor's HVAC system. This teaches us that third-party access is a critical vulnerability. We must audit vendor security and limit their system access."
> 

**Interview tip**: Don't just name the attack — explain the lesson

---

## ⚠️ Common Exam Trap

**Wrong thinking**:

- ❌ "Advanced hacking techniques cause breaches"

**Correct understanding**:

- ✅ "Basic mistakes and weak controls cause major attacks"
- ✅ "Most attacks are preventable with proper security"
