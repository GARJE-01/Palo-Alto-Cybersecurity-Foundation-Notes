# Topic 3: Ransomware (Detailed)

> Malware that locks your data and demands payment for unlock
> 

---

## 📖 SIMPLE EXPLANATION

**Ransomware = Malware that locks your data and asks money to unlock it**

👉 **Two main types:**

- **Locker Ransomware**: Locks the entire system
- **Crypto Ransomware**: Encrypts files

👉 **Data stays locked until ransom paid (hopefully)**

---

## 🌍 REAL-WORLD EXAMPLE

### WannaCry Attack (2017)

- Spread worldwide
- Affected hospitals, companies, governments
- Data encrypted → demanded Bitcoin

👉 **Infected 230,000+ systems globally**

**Impact:**

- Shut down hospitals
- Delayed surgeries
- Financial losses in billions

---

## ⚙️ HOW RANSOMWARE WORKS (STEP-BY-STEP)

### Step 1: Infection

- User clicks phishing email/link
- Downloads malicious file
- Opens infected attachment

### Step 2: Access & Control

- Malware installed
- Gains system access
- Sometimes escalates privileges

### Step 3: Data Encryption

- Malware begins encrypting files
- All important files locked
- Recovery key stored with attacker

### Step 4: Ransom Demand

- Ransom message displayed
- "Your files are encrypted"
- "Pay Bitcoin to recover"
- Timer often included (pressure)

### Step 5: Payment & Recovery (?)

- Victim pays cryptocurrency
- MAYBE gets decryption key
- **No guarantee of recovery**

---

## 퉪 ⚠️ CRITICAL POINT

### Even after paying:

- ❌ **No guarantee of recovery**
- ❌ **No guarantee attacker will respond**
- ❌ **Likely to be attacked again**
- ✅ **Only option: restore from backup**

---

## 🧠 WHY ATTACKERS LOVE RANSOMWARE

### 1. Easy Money 💰

- Direct revenue
- Cryptocurrency = untraceable
- High success rate

### 2. High Success Rate

- Many victims pay
- Less risky than selling data
- Predictable payment

### 3. Business Model

- Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS)
- Criminals sell tools
- Share profits

### 4. Low Risk

- Cryptocurrency hides identity
- Difficult to prosecute
- No need to sell stolen data

---

| Impact | Consequence |

| 🖥️ **Stops business** | Operations halted |

## 🔑 KEY TERMS

| Term | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| **Encryption** | Locking data with cryptographic key |
| **Crypto Ransomware** | Encrypts files |
| **Locker Ransomware** | Locks system screen |
| **Decryption Key** | Code to unlock files |
| **Ransom Note** | Message demanding payment |
| **Ransom Amount** | Bitcoin/payment requested |
| **Recovery Point** | Latest system backup |

## 📊 FAMOUS RANSOMWARE EXAMPLES

### WannaCry (2017)

- Spread via EternalBlue exploit
- 230,000+ infections
- Demanded $300-600 Bitcoin

### NotPetya (2017)

- Similar to WannaCry
- More destructive
- Harder to decrypt

### REvil / Sodinokibi (2019-2021)

- Targeted businesses
- Demanded millions
- Leaked data if not paid

### DarkSide (2020-2021)

- Targeted critical infrastructure
- Colonial Pipeline attack
- Shutdown national fuel supply

## 🎯 HOW TO PREVENT RANSOMWARE

### Technical Controls:

- ✅ Regular backups (offline)
- ✅ Security patches
- ✅ EDR (Endpoint Detection)
- ✅ Network segmentation
- ✅ Email filtering

### Organizational:

- ✅ Employee training
- ✅ Incident response plan
- ✅ Backup testing
- ✅ Disaster recovery plan
- ✅ Backup isolated from network

### Best Practices:

- ✅ 3-2-1 backup rule (3 copies, 2 media, 1 offsite)
- ✅ Immutable backups
- ✅ Test recovery regularly
- ✅ Assume breach mentality

## 💡 INTERVIEW TIP

### 🔥 Question:

**"Why is ransomware so popular with attackers?"**

### ✅ Best Answer:

**"Ransomware directly generates revenue through cryptocurrency payments, has a high success rate since victims often pay to recover critical data, and is difficult to trace due to anonymous blockchain transactions. It's essentially a criminal business model that requires no technical sophistication to deploy through ransomware-as-a-service platforms."**

## 📝 PRACTICE QUESTIONS

### Q1: Prevention

**What is the BEST defense against ransomware?**

A) Pay ransom immediately

B) Regular offline backups

C) Buy more antivirus

D) Disconnect from internet

✅ **Answer: B** - Backups make encryption irrelevant

### Q2: Detection

**What is the first sign of ransomware?**

A) Slow computer

B) Files become inaccessible + ransom note

C) Increased CPU usage

D) Network activity

✅ **Answer: B** - Ransom note is clear indicator

### Q3: Response

**Should you pay ransomware demand?**

A) Always pay to recover

B) Never pay; restore from backup

C) Pay if insurance covers

D) Pay after police approval

✅ **Answer: B** - Paying funds criminals
