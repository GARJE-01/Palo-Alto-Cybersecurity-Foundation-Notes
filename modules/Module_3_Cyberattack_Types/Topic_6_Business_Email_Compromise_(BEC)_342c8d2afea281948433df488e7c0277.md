# Topic 6: Business Email Compromise (BEC)

> Email-based fraud targeting organizations
> 

---

## 📖 SIMPLE EXPLANATION

**BEC = Attacker uses email to trick employees into sending money or sensitive data**

👉 **No malware needed sometimes**

👉 **Pure social engineering attack**

### Real-World Example:

Employee receives email:

- "I am your CEO, urgently transfer ₹50,00,000"
- Looks real, uses correct name
- Employee sends money ❌

---

## 🧠 TYPES OF BEC ATTACKS

### 🔴 1. PHISHING

- Fake email
- Generic message
- Low targeting
- **Example**: "Confirm your password"

### 🔵 2. SPEAR PHISHING

- Targeted email
- Uses personal info
- Higher success rate
- **Example**: "John, update your account in system X"

### 🔶 3. WHALING

- Targets executives
- High-value attacks
- CEO-level targeting
- **Example**: Fake CEO email for wire transfer

### 🔷 4. WATERING HOLE

- Compromises websites
- Infects visitors
- Supply chain attack
- **Example**: Malware on industry website

### 🔺 5. PHARMING

- Redirects real website to fake
- DNS hijacking
- User sees fake login page
- **Example**: Fake bank site

---

## ⚙️ HOW BEC WORKS (STEP-BY-STEP)

### Step 1: Information Gathering

- Research target organization
- Identify employees
- Study company structure
- Find email patterns

### Step 2: Email Crafting

- Create realistic email
- Use similar domain (lookalike)
- Copy CEO/manager style
- Add urgency

### Step 3: Sending

- Send to target employee
- Often to finance/accounting
- May spoofed email address

### Step 4: Building Trust

- Email appears legitimate
- Correct formatting
- Matches communication style
- Creates false urgency

### Step 5: Money/Data Request

- "Transfer funds immediately"
- "Send wire to this account"
- "Provide password"
- "Share sensitive files"

### Step 6: Victim Response

- Employee complies
- Money sent
- Data shared
- Damage done

---

## 📊 BEC STATISTICS

- **$2.9 billion** reported losses
- **88%** of organizations targeted
- **35%** report successful attacks
- Average loss per incident: **$180,000**
- CEO fraud = highest loss

---

## ❓ WHY IT MATTERS

👉 **Causes huge financial loss**

👉 **Hard to detect**

👉 **Targets human behavior (weakest link)**

👉 **No malware = antivirus fails**

---

## 📊 DETECTION SIGNS

### Email Red Flags:

- Slight domain misspelling
- Unusual request for employee
- High urgency/pressure
- Wire transfer requests
- Unusual file requests
- Links to strange sites

---

## 🎯 HOW TO PREVENT

### Technical:

- Email authentication (DMARC)
- Spam filtering
- Link scanning
- Domain validation

### Organizational:

- Employee training
- Verification procedures
- Approval workflows
- Incident reporting

### Best Practices:

- Verify requests out-of-band
- Never wire based on email
- Confirm with phone call
- Check sender email carefully
