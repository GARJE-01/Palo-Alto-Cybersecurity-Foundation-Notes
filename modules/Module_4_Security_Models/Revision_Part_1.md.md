## 🎯 Key Questions

### Q1: Zero Trust Concept

**What does "Never Trust, Always Verify" mean?**

✅ **Answer:** No user or device is trusted by default; every request must be authenticated and authorized

### Q2: Principles

**What are the three core principles?**

✅ **Answer:** Verify Everything, Least Privilege Access, Inspect & Monitor All Traffic

### Q3: Attack Surface

**Why focus on protect surface instead of attack surface?**

✅ **Answer:** Attack surface too large to protect entirely; focus on critical assets is effective

### Q4: Architecture

**What does micro-segmentation prevent?**

✅ **Answer:** Lateral movement; limits breach scope to one zone

### Q5: Benefits

**Why is Zero Trust important for remote work?**

✅ **Answer:** Works regardless of device location; verifies everywhere

## 📝 Practice Questions

### Q1: Definition

**Zero Trust means:**

A) Trust all internal users

B) Never trust, always verify

C) Trust devices automatically

D) Trust after login

✅ **Answer: B**

### Q2: Principles

**Which is NOT a Zero Trust principle?**

A) Verify everything

B) Least privilege

C) Trust perimeter

D) Monitor all traffic

✅ **Answer: C** - Perimeter doesn't exist

### Q3: Architecture

**Micro-segmentation prevents:**

A) Slow networks

B) Lateral movement

C) User errors

D) Software bugs

✅ **Answer: B**

### Q4: Advantage

**Zero Trust works best with:**

A) Traditional office only

B) Large perimeter

C) Cloud + remote work

D) No monitoring

✅ **Answer: C**

### Q5: DAAS

**Protect surface includes:**

A) Entire network

B) All devices

C) Data, Assets, Apps, Services

D) All users

✅ **Answer: C**
